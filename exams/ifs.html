Indian Forest Service

The Indian Forest Service (IFS) Examination is one of the three All India Services, along with the Indian Administrative Service (IAS) and the Indian Police Service (IPS). The IFS exam is conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) for the recruitment of officers for the forest services of India. These officers are responsible for managing and protecting the country's forest resources.

Official Website:

Indian Forest Service

Exam Pattern

1.Preliminary Examination


The Preliminary Examination is the same as the Civil Services Examination (CSE) Prelims, and it is used to shortlist candidates for the IFS Main Examination.
Mode: Offline
Papers: Two
General Studies Paper I: 200 marks
General Studies Paper II (CSAT): 200 marks (Qualifying, 33% marks required)
Duration: 2 hours each

2.Main Examination:


Candidates who qualify for the Preliminary Examination are eligible to appear for the IFS Main Examination.
Mode: Offline
Papers: Six
Paper I (General English): 300 marks
Paper II (General Knowledge): 300 marks
Papers III, IV, V, and VI (Optional Subjects): 200 marks each
Candidates have to choose two subjects from the list of optional subjects, and each subject will have two papers.

3.Personality Test (Interview)


Candidates who qualify for the Main Examination are called for the Personality Test.
Marks: 300

Eligibility Criteria

1.Nationality:
A candidate must be a citizen of India, or a subject of Nepal, or a subject of Bhutan, or a Tibetan refugee who came to India before January 1, 1962, with the intention of permanently settling in India.
A person of Indian origin who has migrated from Pakistan, Burma, Sri Lanka, and East African countries with the intention of permanently settling in India.

2.Age Limit:
Minimum Age: 21 years
Maximum Age: 32 years
Age relaxation is provided for candidates belonging to certain categories (e.g., SC/ST, OBC, PwD, etc.).

3.Educational Qualification:
A candidate must hold a Bachelor's degree with at least one of the following subjects: Animal Husbandry & Veterinary Science, Botany, Chemistry, Geology, Mathematics, Physics, Statistics, and Zoology.
Alternatively, a Bachelor's degree in Agriculture, Forestry, or Engineering is also acceptable.
