COMBINED DEFENCE SERVICES

The Combined Defence Services (CDS) Examination is conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) for recruitment into the Indian Military Academy (IMA), Indian Naval Academy (INA), Air Force Academy (AFA), and Officers Training Academy (OTA). The exam is held twice a year, typically in February and November.

Official Website:

Combined Defence Services

CDS Exam Pattern

Exam Structure:


1.For IMA, INA, and AFA:
Duration: 2 hours for each paper
Total Marks: 300
English: 100 marks
General Knowledge: 100 marks
Elementary Mathematics: 100 marks
2.For OTA:
Duration: 2 hours for each paper
Total Marks: 200
English: 100 marks
General Knowledge: 100 marks

Subjects and Syllabus

1.English:
Objective type questions to test comprehension, correct usage, and understanding of the English language.
Syllabus includes grammar, vocabulary, comprehension, and sentence structure.

2.General Knowledge:
Questions cover current events, Indian history, geography, politics, economics, and other general topics.
Includes questions on the Constitution of India, social systems, scientific developments, and more.

3.Elementary Mathematics (for IMA, INA, AFA):
Arithmetic: Number systems, elementary number theory, ratios and proportions, averages, etc.
Algebra: Basic operations, simple factors, HCF and LCM, quadratic equations, etc.
Geometry: Lines and angles, plane and plane figures, theorems, triangles, circles, etc.
Trigonometry: Trigonometric ratios, use of trigonometric tables, simple problems.
Mensuration: Areas and volumes, surface areas, volumes of cubes, cuboids, spheres, etc.
Statistics: Collection and tabulation of data, measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode).

Selection Process

1.Written Examination: Candidates need to qualify in each subject to be considered for the next stage.

2.Services Selection Board (SSB) Interview: Candidates who qualify in the written exam are called for an interview conducted by the SSB. This stage includes psychological tests, group discussions, personal interviews, and physical fitness tests.

3.Medical Examination: Candidates who clear the SSB interview undergo a medical examination to ensure they meet the required health and fitness standards.

4.Merit List: A final merit list is prepared based on the candidateâ€™s performance in the written exam and SSB interview. Successful candidates are then selected for training at their respective academies.

Eligibility Criteria

1.Nationality:
A candidate must be a citizen of India, or a subject of Bhutan, or a subject of Nepal, or a Tibetan refugee who came to India before January 1, 1962, with the intention of permanently settling in India.
A person of Indian origin who has migrated from Pakistan, Burma, Sri Lanka, and East African countries with the intention of permanently settling in India.

2.Age Limit and Marital Status:
Indian Military Academy (IMA): Unmarried males, 19-24 years.
Indian Naval Academy (INA): Unmarried males, 19-24 years.
Air Force Academy (AFA): Unmarried males, 20-24 years.
Officers Training Academy (OTA): (Men) Unmarried males, 19-25 years.
Officers Training Academy (OTA): (Women) Unmarried females, widows, and divorcees (who have not remarried), 19-25 years.

3.Educational Qualifications:
IMA and OTA: A degree from a recognized University or equivalent.
INA: A degree in Engineering from a recognized University/Institution.
AFA: A degree from a recognized University (with Physics and Mathematics at 10+2 level) or a Bachelor of Engineering.
