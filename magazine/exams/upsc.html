Union Public Service Commission

The Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) exam, commonly referred to as the Civil Services Examination (CSE), is one of the most prestigious and challenging exams in India. It is conducted annually by the UPSC to recruit candidates for various civil services of the Government of India, including the Indian Administrative Service (IAS), Indian Police Service (IPS), Indian Foreign Service (IFS), and other central services.

Fact #1:

The civil services include three types of services namely, the All India Services, Group A and Group B Central Services.

UPSC Exam Pattern

1.Preliminary Examination (Prelims):

Objective Type: Consists of two papers of 200 marks each.
General Studies Paper I: Covers subjects like history, geography, economics, polity, environment, current affairs, and general science.
General Studies Paper II (CSAT): Tests logical reasoning, analytical ability, decision-making, problem-solving, basic numeracy, and English language comprehension skills.
Qualifying Nature: Only the marks of General Studies Paper I are considered for shortlisting candidates for the Mains exam. Paper II is qualifying in nature and requires a minimum of 33% to pass.

2.Main Examination (Mains):

Descriptive Type: Consists of nine papers, out of which seven are considered for the final ranking, and two are qualifying in nature.
Qualifying Papers:
Paper A: Indian language (selected by the candidate)
Paper B: English
Papers Counted for Merit:
Essay
General Studies I: Indian Heritage and Culture, History, and Geography of the World and Society
General Studies II: Governance, Constitution, Polity, Social Justice, and International Relations
General Studies III: Technology, Economic Development, Biodiversity, Environment, Security, and Disaster Management
General Studies IV: Ethics, Integrity, and Aptitude
Optional Subject Paper I: Subject chosen by the candidate
Optional Subject Paper II: Subject chosen by the candidate

3.Personality Test (Interview):

Conducted by a Board:
The interview panel assesses the candidate's personality, leadership qualities, communication skills, and overall suitability for a career in civil services.
Marks: The interview carries 275 marks.

Eligibility Criteria

The prescribed UPSC exam eligibility criteria are that the candidate should be a graduate and must have attained 21 years of age. There is also an upper age limit and a number of attempts restriction based on the candidate’s category. Generally, the upper age limit is 32 years, but the commission gives age relaxation for OBC, SC, ST and PH candidates.
